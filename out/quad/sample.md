\chapter{Genesis}





\subsection*{Chapter 1}
\chapmark{Genesis 1}



{\scriptsize ₁} In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth. {\scriptsize ₂} And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters.

{\scriptsize ₃} And God said, Let there be light: and there was light. {\scriptsize ₄} And God saw the light, that it was good: and God divided the light from the darkness. {\scriptsize ₅} And God called the light Day, and the darkness he called Night. And the evening and the morning were the first day.

{\scriptsize ₆} And God said, Let there be a firmament in the midst of the waters, and let it divide the waters from the waters. {\scriptsize ₇} And God made the firmament, and divided the waters which were under the firmament from the waters which were above the firmament: and it was so. {\scriptsize ₈} And God called the firmament Heaven. And the evening and the morning were the second day.

{\scriptsize ₉} And God said, Let the waters under the heaven be gathered together unto one place, and let the dry land appear: and it was so. {\scriptsize ₁₀} And God called the dry land Earth; and the gathering together of the waters called he Seas: and God saw that it was good. {\scriptsize ₁₁} And God said, Let the earth bring forth grass, the herb yielding seed, and the fruit tree yielding fruit after his kind, whose seed is in itself, upon the earth: and it was so. {\scriptsize ₁₂} And the earth brought forth grass, and herb yielding seed after his kind, and the tree yielding fruit, whose seed was in itself, after his kind: and God saw that it was good. {\scriptsize ₁₃} And the evening and the morning were the third day.

{\scriptsize ₁₄} And God said, Let there be lights in the firmament of the heaven to divide the day from the night; and let them be for signs, and for seasons, and for days, and years: {\scriptsize ₁₅} and let them be for lights in the firmament of the heaven to give light upon the earth: and it was so. {\scriptsize ₁₆} And God made two great lights; the greater light to rule the day, and the lesser light to rule the night: he made the stars also. {\scriptsize ₁₇} And God set them in the firmament of the heaven to give light upon the earth, {\scriptsize ₁₈} and to rule over the day and over the night, and to divide the light from the darkness: and God saw that it was good. {\scriptsize ₁₉} And the evening and the morning were the fourth day.

{\scriptsize ₂₀} And God said, Let the waters bring forth abundantly the moving creature that hath life, and fowl that may fly above the earth in the open firmament of heaven. {\scriptsize ₂₁} And God created great whales, and every living creature that moveth, which the waters brought forth abundantly, after their kind, and every winged fowl after his kind: and God saw that it was good. {\scriptsize ₂₂} And God blessed them, saying, Be fruitful, and multiply, and fill the waters in the seas, and let fowl multiply in the earth. {\scriptsize ₂₃} And the evening and the morning were the fifth day.

{\scriptsize ₂₄} And God said, Let the earth bring forth the living creature after his kind, cattle, and creeping thing, and beast of the earth after his kind: and it was so. {\scriptsize ₂₅} And God made the beast of the earth after his kind, and cattle after their kind, and every thing that creepeth upon the earth after his kind: and God saw that it was good.

{\scriptsize ₂₆} And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over the cattle, and over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth. {\scriptsize ₂₇} So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him; male and female created he them. {\scriptsize ₂₈} And God blessed them, and God said unto them, Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth, and subdue it: and have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over every living thing that moveth upon the earth.

{\scriptsize ₂₉} And God said, Behold, I have given you every herb bearing seed, which is upon the face of all the earth, and every tree, in the which is the fruit of a tree yielding seed; to you it shall be for meat. {\scriptsize ₃₀} And to every beast of the earth, and to every fowl of the air, and to every thing that creepeth upon the earth, wherein there is life, I have given every green herb for meat: and it was so. {\scriptsize ₃₁} And God saw every thing that he had made, and behold, it was very good. And the evening and the morning were the sixth day.



\subsection*{Chapter 2}
\chapmark{Genesis 2}



{\scriptsize ₁} Thus the heavens and the earth were finished, and all the host of them. {\scriptsize ₂} And on the seventh day God ended his work which he had made; and he rested on the seventh day from all his work which he had made. {\scriptsize ₃} And God blessed the seventh day, and sanctified it: because that in it he had rested from all his work which God created and made.

{\scriptsize ₄} These are the generations of the heavens and of the earth when they were created, in the day that the Lord God made the earth and the heavens, {\scriptsize ₅} and every plant of the field before it was in the earth, and every herb of the field before it grew: for the Lord God had not caused it to rain upon the earth, and there was not a man to till the ground. {\scriptsize ₆} But there went up a mist from the earth, and watered the whole face of the ground. {\scriptsize ₇} And the Lord God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul.

{\scriptsize ₈} And the Lord God planted a garden eastward in Eden; and there he put the man whom he had formed. {\scriptsize ₉} And out of the ground made the Lord God to grow every tree that is pleasant to the sight, and good for food; the tree of life also in the midst of the garden, and the tree of knowledge of good and evil. {\scriptsize ₁₀} And a river went out of Eden to water the garden; and from thence it was parted, and became into four heads. {\scriptsize ₁₁} The name of the first is Pison: that is it which compasseth the whole land of Havilah, where there is gold; {\scriptsize ₁₂} and the gold of that land is good: there is bdellium and the onyx stone. {\scriptsize ₁₃} And the name of the second river is Gihon: the same is it that compasseth the whole land of Ethiopia. {\scriptsize ₁₄} And the name of the third river is Hiddekel: that is it which goeth toward the east of Assyria. And the fourth river is Euphrates. {\scriptsize ₁₅} And the Lord God took the man, and put him into the garden of Eden to dress it and to keep it. {\scriptsize ₁₆} And the Lord God commanded the man, saying, Of every tree of the garden thou mayest freely eat: {\scriptsize ₁₇} but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it: for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die.

{\scriptsize ₁₈} And the Lord God said, It is not good that the man should be alone; I will make him an help meet for him. {\scriptsize ₁₉} And out of the ground the Lord God formed every beast of the field, and every fowl of the air; and brought them unto Adam to see what he would call them: and whatsoever Adam called every living creature, that was the name thereof. {\scriptsize ₂₀} And Adam gave names to all cattle, and to the fowl of the air, and to every beast of the field; but for Adam there was not found an help meet for him. {\scriptsize ₂₁} And the Lord God caused a deep sleep to fall upon Adam, and he slept: and he took one of his ribs, and closed up the flesh instead thereof; {\scriptsize ₂₂} and the rib, which the Lord God had taken from man, made he a woman, and brought her unto the man. {\scriptsize ₂₃} And Adam said, This is now bone of my bones, and flesh of my flesh: she shall be called Woman, because she was taken out of Man. {\scriptsize ₂₄} Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh. {\scriptsize ₂₅} And they were both naked, the man and his wife, and were not ashamed.



\subsection*{Chapter 3}
\chapmark{Genesis 3}



{\scriptsize ₁} Now the serpent was more subtil than any beast of the field which the Lord God had made. And he said unto the woman, Yea, hath God said, Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden? {\scriptsize ₂} And the woman said unto the serpent, We may eat of the fruit of the trees of the garden: {\scriptsize ₃} but of the fruit of the tree which is in the midst of the garden, God hath said, Ye shall not eat of it, neither shall ye touch it, lest ye die. {\scriptsize ₄} And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die: {\scriptsize ₅} for God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof, then your eyes shall be opened, and ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil. {\scriptsize ₆} And when the woman saw that the tree was good for food, and that it was pleasant to the eyes, and a tree to be desired to make one wise, she took of the fruit thereof, and did eat, and gave also unto her husband with her; and he did eat. {\scriptsize ₇} And the eyes of them both were opened, and they knew that they were naked; and they sewed fig leaves together, and made themselves aprons. {\scriptsize ₈} And they heard the voice of the Lord God walking in the garden in the cool of the day: and Adam and his wife hid themselves from the presence of the Lord God amongst the trees of the garden. {\scriptsize ₉} And the Lord God called unto Adam, and said unto him, Where art thou? {\scriptsize ₁₀} And he said, I heard thy voice in the garden, and I was afraid, because I was naked; and I hid myself. {\scriptsize ₁₁} And he said, Who told thee that thou wast naked? Hast thou eaten of the tree, whereof I commanded thee that thou shouldest not eat? {\scriptsize ₁₂} And the man said, The woman whom thou gavest to be with me, she gave me of the tree, and I did eat. {\scriptsize ₁₃} And the Lord God said unto the woman, What is this that thou hast done? And the woman said, The serpent beguiled me, and I did eat. {\scriptsize ₁₄} And the Lord God said unto the serpent, Because thou hast done this, thou art cursed above all cattle, and above every beast of the field; upon thy belly shalt thou go, and dust shalt thou eat all the days of thy life: {\scriptsize ₁₅} and I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel. {\scriptsize ₁₆} Unto the woman he said, I will greatly multiply thy sorrow and thy conception; in sorrow thou shalt bring forth children; and thy desire shall be to thy husband, and he shall rule over thee. {\scriptsize ₁₇} And unto Adam he said, Because thou hast hearkened unto the voice of thy wife, and hast eaten of the tree, of which I commanded thee, saying, Thou shalt not eat of it: cursed is the ground for thy sake; in sorrow shalt thou eat of it all the days of thy life; {\scriptsize ₁₈} thorns also and thistles shall it bring forth to thee; and thou shalt eat the herb of the field; {\scriptsize ₁₉} in the sweat of thy face shalt thou eat bread, till thou return unto the ground; for out of it wast thou taken: for dust thou art, and unto dust shalt thou return. {\scriptsize ₂₀} And Adam called his wife's name Eve; because she was the mother of all living. {\scriptsize ₂₁} Unto Adam also and to his wife did the Lord God make coats of skins, and clothed them. {\scriptsize ₂₂} And the Lord God said, Behold, the man is become as one of us, to know good and evil: and now, lest he put forth his hand, and take also of the tree of life, and eat, and live for ever: {\scriptsize ₂₃} therefore the Lord God sent him forth from the garden of Eden, to till the ground from whence he was taken. {\scriptsize ₂₄} So he drove out the man; and he placed at the east of the garden of Eden Cherubims, and a flaming sword which turned every way, to keep the way of the tree of life.



\subsection*{Chapter 4}


\chapter{1 Nephi}





\invisiblechapter{1 Nephi 1}

{\scriptsize ₁} I, Nephi, having been born of goodly parents, therefore I was taught somewhat in all the learning of my father; and having seen many afflictions in the course of my days, nevertheless, having been highly favored of the Lord in all my days; yea, having had a great knowledge of the goodness and the mysteries of God, therefore I make a record of my proceedings in my days. {\scriptsize ₂} Yea, I make a record in the language of my father, which consists of the learning of the Jews and the language of the Egyptians. {\scriptsize ₃} And I know that the record which I make is true; and I make it with mine own hand; and I make it according to my knowledge.

{\scriptsize ₄} For it came to pass in the commencement of the first year of the reign of Zedekiah, king of Judah, (my father, Lehi, having dwelt at Jerusalem in all his days); and in that same year there came many prophets, prophesying unto the people that they must repent, or the great city Jerusalem must be destroyed. {\scriptsize ₅} Wherefore it came to pass that my father, Lehi, as he went forth prayed unto the Lord, yea, even with all his heart, in behalf of his people.

{\scriptsize ₆} And it came to pass as he prayed unto the Lord, there came a pillar of fire and dwelt upon a rock before him; and he saw and heard much; and because of the things which he saw and heard he did quake and tremble exceedingly.

{\scriptsize ₇} And it came to pass that he returned to his own house at Jerusalem; and he cast himself upon his bed, being overcome with the Spirit and the things which he had seen. {\scriptsize ₈} And being thus overcome with the Spirit, he was carried away in a vision, even that he saw the heavens open, and he thought he saw God sitting upon his throne, surrounded with numberless concourses of angels in the attitude of singing and praising their God.

{\scriptsize ₉} And it came to pass that he saw One descending out of the midst of heaven, and he beheld that his luster was above that of the sun at noon-day. {\scriptsize ₁₀} And he also saw twelve others following him, and their brightness did exceed that of the stars in the firmament. {\scriptsize ₁₁} And they came down and went forth upon the face of the earth; and the first came and stood before my father, and gave unto him a book, and bade him that he should read.

{\scriptsize ₁₂} And it came to pass that as he read, he was filled with the Spirit of the Lord. {\scriptsize ₁₃} And he read, saying: Wo, wo, unto Jerusalem, for I have seen thine abominations! Yea, and many things did my father read concerning Jerusalem—that it should be destroyed, and the inhabitants thereof; many should perish by the sword, and many should be carried away captive into Babylon.

{\scriptsize ₁₄} And it came to pass that when my father had read and seen many great and marvelous things, he did exclaim many things unto the Lord; such as: Great and marvelous are thy works, O Lord God Almighty! Thy throne is high in the heavens, and thy power, and goodness, and mercy are over all the inhabitants of the earth; and, because thou art merciful, thou wilt not suffer those who come unto thee that they shall perish! {\scriptsize ₁₅} And after this manner was the language of my father in the praising of his God; for his soul did rejoice, and his whole heart was filled, because of the things which he had seen, yea, which the Lord had shown unto him.

{\scriptsize ₁₆} And now I, Nephi, do not make a full account of the things which my father hath written, for he hath written many things which he saw in visions and in dreams; and he also hath written many things which he prophesied and spake unto his children, of which I shall not make a full account. {\scriptsize ₁₇} But I shall make an account of my proceedings in my days. Behold, I make an abridgment of the record of my father, upon plates which I have made with mine own hands; wherefore, after I have abridged the record of my father then will I make an account of mine own life.

{\scriptsize ₁₈} Therefore, I would that ye should know, that after the Lord had shown so many marvelous things unto my father, Lehi, yea, concerning the destruction of Jerusalem, behold he went forth among the people, and began to prophesy and to declare unto them concerning the things which he had both seen and heard.

{\scriptsize ₁₉} And it came to pass that the Jews did mock him because of the things which he testified of them; for he truly testified of their wickedness and their abominations; and he testified that the things which he saw and heard, and also the things which he read in the book, manifested plainly of the coming of a Messiah, and also the redemption of the world. {\scriptsize ₂₀} And when the Jews heard these things they were angry with him; yea, even as with the prophets of old, whom they had cast out, and stoned, and slain; and they also sought his life, that they might take it away. But behold, I, Nephi, will show unto you that the tender mercies of the Lord are over all those whom he hath chosen, because of their faith, to make them mighty even unto the power of deliverance.



\invisiblechapter{1 Nephi 2}

{\scriptsize ₁} For behold, it came to pass that the Lord spake unto my father, yea, even in a dream, and said unto him: Blessed art thou Lehi, because of the things which thou hast done; and because thou hast been faithful and declared unto this people the things which I commanded thee, behold, they seek to take away thy life.

{\scriptsize ₂} And it came to pass that the Lord commanded my father, even in a dream, that he should take his family and depart into the wilderness. {\scriptsize ₃} And it came to pass that he was obedient unto the word of the Lord, wherefore he did as the Lord commanded him.

{\scriptsize ₄} And it came to pass that he departed into the wilderness. And he left his house, and the land of his inheritance, and his gold, and his silver, and his precious things, and took nothing with him, save it were his family, and provisions, and tents, and departed into the wilderness. {\scriptsize ₅} And he came down by the borders near the shore of the Red Sea; and he traveled in the wilderness in the borders which are nearer the Red Sea; and he did travel in the wilderness with his family, which consisted of my mother, Sariah, and my elder brothers, who were Laman, Lemuel, and Sam.

{\scriptsize ₆} And it came to pass that when he had traveled three days in the wilderness, he pitched his tent in a valley by the side of a river of water. {\scriptsize ₇} And it came to pass that he built an altar of stones, and made an offering unto the Lord, and gave thanks unto the Lord our God.

{\scriptsize ₈} And it came to pass that he called the name of the river, Laman, and it emptied into the Red Sea; and the valley was in the borders near the mouth thereof. {\scriptsize ₉} And when my father saw that the waters of the river emptied into the fountain of the Red Sea, he spake unto Laman, saying: O that thou mightest be like unto this river, continually running into the fountain of all righteousness!

{\scriptsize ₁₀} And he also spake unto Lemuel: O that thou mightest be like unto this valley, firm and steadfast, and immovable in keeping the commandments of the Lord!

{\scriptsize ₁₁} Now this he spake because of the stiffneckedness of Laman and Lemuel; for behold they did murmur in many things against their father, because he was a visionary man, and had led them out of the land of Jerusalem, to leave the land of their inheritance, and their gold, and their silver, and their precious things, to perish in the wilderness. And this they said he had done because of the foolish imaginations of his heart. {\scriptsize ₁₂} And thus Laman and Lemuel, being the eldest, did murmur against their father. And they did murmur because they knew not the dealings of that God who had created them. {\scriptsize ₁₃} Neither did they believe that Jerusalem, that great city, could be destroyed according to the words of the prophets. And they were like unto the Jews who were at Jerusalem, who sought to take away the life of my father.

{\scriptsize ₁₄} And it came to pass that my father did speak unto them in the valley of Lemuel, with power, being filled with the Spirit, until their frames did shake before him. And he did confound them, that they durst not utter against him; wherefore, they did as he commanded them.

{\scriptsize ₁₅} And my father dwelt in a tent. {\scriptsize ₁₆} And it came to pass that I, Nephi, being exceedingly young, nevertheless being large in stature, and also having great desires to know of the mysteries of God, wherefore, I did cry unto the Lord; and behold he did visit me, and did soften my heart that I did believe all the words which had been spoken by my father; wherefore, I did not rebel against him like unto my brothers. {\scriptsize ₁₇} And I spake unto Sam, making known unto him the things which the Lord had manifested unto me by his Holy Spirit. And it came to pass that he believed in my words. {\scriptsize ₁₈} But, behold, Laman and Lemuel would not hearken unto my words; and being grieved because of the hardness of their hearts I cried unto the Lord for them.

{\scriptsize ₁₉} And it came to pass that the Lord spake unto me, saying: Blessed art thou, Nephi, because of thy faith, for thou hast sought me diligently, with lowliness of heart. {\scriptsize ₂₀} And inasmuch as ye shall keep my commandments, ye shall prosper, and shall be led to a land of promise; yea, even a land which I have prepared for you; yea, a land which is choice above all other lands. {\scriptsize ₂₁} And inasmuch as thy brethren shall rebel against thee, they shall be cut off from the presence of the Lord. {\scriptsize ₂₂} And inasmuch as thou shalt keep my commandments, thou shalt be made a ruler and a teacher over thy brethren. {\scriptsize ₂₃} For behold, in that day that they shall rebel against me, I will curse them even with a sore curse, and they shall have no power over thy seed except they shall rebel against me also. {\scriptsize ₂₄} And if it so be that they rebel against me, they shall be a scourge unto thy seed, to stir them up in the ways of remembrance.



\invisiblechapter{1 Nephi 3}

{\scriptsize ₁} And it came to pass that I, Nephi, returned from speaking with the Lord, to the tent of my father. {\scriptsize ₂} And it came to pass that he spake unto me, saying: Behold I have dreamed a dream, in the which the Lord hath commanded me that thou and thy brethren shall return to Jerusalem. {\scriptsize ₃} For behold, Laban hath the record of the Jews and also a genealogy of my forefathers, and they are engraven upon plates of brass. {\scriptsize ₄} Wherefore, the Lord hath commanded me that thou and thy brothers should go unto the house of Laban, and seek the records, and bring them down hither into the wilderness. {\scriptsize ₅} And now, behold thy brothers murmur, saying it is a hard thing which I have required of them; but behold I have not required it of them, but it is a commandment of the Lord. {\scriptsize ₆} Therefore go, my son, and thou shalt be favored of the Lord, because thou hast not murmured.

{\scriptsize ₇} And it came to pass that I, Nephi, said unto my father: I will go and do the things which the Lord hath commanded, for I know that the Lord giveth no commandments unto the children of men, save he shall prepare a way for them that they may accomplish the thing which he commandeth them.

{\scriptsize ₈} And it came to pass that when my father had heard these words he was exceedingly glad, for he knew that I had been blessed of the Lord. {\scriptsize ₉} And I, Nephi, and my brethren took our journey in the wilderness, with our tents, to go up to the land of Jerusalem.

{\scriptsize ₁₀} And it came to pass that when we had gone up to the land of Jerusalem, I and my brethren did consult one with another. {\scriptsize ₁₁} And we cast lots—who of us should go in unto the house of Laban. And it came to pass that the lot fell upon Laman; and Laman went in unto the house of Laban, and he talked with him as he sat in his house. {\scriptsize ₁₂} And he desired of Laban the records which were engraven upon the plates of brass, which contained the genealogy of my father.

{\scriptsize ₁₃} And behold, it came to pass that Laban was angry, and thrust him out from his presence; and he would not that he should have the records. Wherefore, he said unto him: Behold thou art a robber, and I will slay thee. {\scriptsize ₁₄} But Laman fled out of his presence, and told the things which Laban had done, unto us. And we began to be exceedingly sorrowful, and my brethren were about to return unto my father in the wilderness.

{\scriptsize ₁₅} But behold I said unto them that: As the Lord liveth, and as we live, we will not go down unto our father in the wilderness until we have accomplished the thing which the Lord hath commanded us. {\scriptsize ₁₆} Wherefore, let us be faithful in keeping the commandments of the Lord; therefore let us go down to the land of our father’s inheritance, for behold he left gold and silver, and all manner of riches. And all this he hath done because of the commandments of the Lord. {\scriptsize ₁₇} For he knew that Jerusalem must be destroyed, because of the wickedness of the people. {\scriptsize ₁₈} For behold, they have rejected the words of the prophets. Wherefore, if my father should dwell in the land after he hath been commanded to flee out of the land, behold, he would also perish. Wherefore, it must needs be that he flee out of the land.

{\scriptsize ₁₉} And behold, it is wisdom in God that we should obtain these records, that we may preserve unto our children the language of our fathers; {\scriptsize ₂₀} and also that we may preserve unto them the words which have been spoken by the mouth of all the holy prophets, which have been delivered unto them by the Spirit and power of God, since the world began, even down unto this present time.

{\scriptsize ₂₁} And it came to pass that after this manner of language did I persuade my brethren, that they might be faithful in keeping the commandments of God. {\scriptsize ₂₂} And it came to pass that we went down to the land of our inheritance, and we did gather together our gold, and our silver, and our precious things.

{\scriptsize ₂₃} And after we had gathered these things together, we went up again unto the house of Laban. {\scriptsize ₂₄} And it came to pass that we went in unto Laban, and desired him that he would give unto us the records which were engraven upon the plates of brass, for which we would give unto him our gold, and our silver, and all our precious things.

{\scriptsize ₂₅} And it came to pass that when Laban saw our property, and that it was exceedingly great, he did lust after it, insomuch that he thrust us out, and sent his servants to slay us, that he might obtain our property. {\scriptsize ₂₆} And it came to pass that we did flee before the servants of Laban, and we were obliged to leave behind our property, and it fell into the hands of Laban.

{\scriptsize ₂₇} And it came to pass that we fled into the wilderness, and the servants of Laban did not overtake us, and we hid ourselves in the cavity of a rock. {\scriptsize ₂₈} And it came to pass that Laman was angry with me, and also with my father; and also was Lemuel, for he hearkened unto the words of Laman. Wherefore Laman and Lemuel did speak many hard words unto us, their younger brothers, and they did smite us even with a rod.

{\scriptsize ₂₉} And it came to pass as they smote us with a rod, behold, an angel of the Lord came and stood before them, and he spake unto them, saying: Why do ye smite your younger brother with a rod? Know ye not that the Lord hath chosen him to be a ruler over you, and this because of your iniquities? Behold ye shall go up to Jerusalem again, and the Lord will deliver Laban into your hands. {\scriptsize ₃₀} And after the angel had spoken unto us, he departed.

{\scriptsize ₃₁} And after the angel had departed, Laman and Lemuel again began to murmur, saying: How is it possible that the Lord will deliver Laban into our hands? Behold, he is a mighty man, and he can command fifty, yea, even he can slay fifty; then why not us?
